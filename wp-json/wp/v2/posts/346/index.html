Rainy day or bright summer morning, if you don\u2019t have access to a nice backyard space, it\u2019s important to have a few fun indoor activities in mind that will keep your kid occupied. Staying in the house can often lead to hours of TV time, which isn\u2019t good for your child.<\/p>\n

It might be better to have some activities to do that will be fun, lead to some learning, and give you some quality time with your child. Here are our favorite indoor activities for kids to enjoy with parents.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Making A Pillow Fort<\/h3>\n

If your kid is stuck inside and feeling low, there’s nothing better than building a pillow fort to help improve their mood. Inside a pillow fort, it feels as if you’re in an entirely different world, and everything starts to feel fun and magical. You can get some sheets from the bed and add fairy lights for an extra touch of magic. Sitting inside the pillow fort and talking with your kid will make them happier than ever.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Baking Some Yummy Treats<\/h3>\n

Baking something with your kid can be a fun and learning experience that your kid will likely remember for a long time. Not only is it extremely rewarding when you go through the process of baking and then get to enjoy the fruit of your labor, but you also get the added benefit of having something delicious to eat for the next few days. Try out some\u00a0<\/span>easy baking recipes<\/a>\u00a0<\/span>with your kid.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Reading Together<\/h3>\n

Reading with your kid will help you form a closer bond with them while also helping them learn and gain valuable skills. Whether you take turns reading aloud to each other or just read while they listen, it can be a great way to stay occupied for hours.<\/p>\n

When it comes to books, there are so many options to choose from. You can try out any genre, from magic and mystery to science fiction or horror.\u00a0<\/span>Here<\/a>\u00a0<\/span>are some books to check out.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Painting Portraits<\/h3>\n

Painting and arts and crafts allow your child’s artistic side to shine through. It is a great way for your kid to channel their creativity and express themselves. A fun interactive way to paint with your kid is to paint portraits of each other! You can sit opposite each other with your canvases and easels, and in the end, do a portrait reveal.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Planting Flowers<\/h3>\n

Planting flowers is one of the most exciting indoor activities for kids because they can keep an eye on the plant and record its growth every day. It is also a great way to teach your child to be more responsible by writing out a watering schedule for them to follow.\u00a0<\/span>Here is a tutorial<\/a>\u00a0<\/span>on how to grow paperwhites indoors.<\/p>\n

Try out these fun indoor activities with your kid and enjoy some quality time spent together. Not only will your child feel closer to you, but they will learn from these activities and have a healthy way to occupy their time.<\/p>\n
